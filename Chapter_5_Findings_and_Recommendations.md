# Chapter 5: Findings and Recommendations

## 5.1 Introduction

This chapter interprets the analytical results from Chapter 4 in the context of the research questions and derives specific, actionable recommendations for UPSC coaching institute managers.

## 5.2 Findings Related to Research Questions

### RQ1: Faculty Load Score and Course Completion Delay

Faculty Load Score is the dominant operational predictor of course completion delay (r = 0.847, p < 0.0001).

- High FLS category (>180): average delay of 13.78 days
- Low FLS category (<120): average delay of 3.83 days
- Insights IAS (FLS = 198.40) had worse delays than La Excellence (FLS = 122.86) despite lower batch pressure

### RQ2: Batch Pressure and Course Completion Delay

Batch Pressure is a significant positive predictor (r = 0.612, p = 0.0088) with threshold at BP = 1.05.

- Low Pressure (<0.95): mean delay 4.71 days
- Normal Pressure (0.95-1.05): mean delay 8.17 days
- High Pressure (>1.05): mean delay 12.31 days

### RQ3: Interaction Between Faculty Load Score and Batch Pressure

Interaction term is significant (beta = -0.1124, p = 0.0081), adding 12% explanatory power (total R2 = 0.9094).

- Best case (Low FLS + Low BP): 4.92 days average delay
- Worst case (High FLS + High BP): 15.40 days average delay

Operational risk is multiplicative, not additive.

### RQ4: Inter-Institute Differences

Significant differences exist (ANOVA F = 4.23, p = 0.027):
- La Excellence: 6.57 days (lowest FLS = 122.86)
- IAS Baba: 9.33 days (intermediate FLS = 148.44)
- Insights IAS: 10.90 days (highest FLS = 198.40)

## 5.3 Operational Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Faculty Load Score Ceiling of 150
Set FLS = 150 as the maximum. Monitor monthly and trigger reallocation review when exceeded.

### Recommendation 2: Cap Batch Pressure at 1.10
When a batch reaches 110% of planned capacity, open a new batch. Build this as a hard admissions system limit.

### Recommendation 3: Operational Risk Score (ORS)
ORS = Batch Pressure x Faculty Load Score. Flag batches with ORS > 200 as high-risk. Trigger proactive interventions.

### Recommendation 4: Dynamic Enrollment Caps
- FLS < 100: BP up to 1.20
- FLS 100-150: BP up to 1.10
- FLS 150-180: BP up to 1.05
- FLS >= 180: BP must be <= 1.00

### Recommendation 5: Benchmark Against La Excellence
- Limit faculty to 5-6 concurrent batch assignments maximum
- Cap weekly teaching hours at 30 per faculty
- Employ specialist faculty rather than generalists

### Recommendation 6: Build 5-7 Day Buffer Into Timelines
As a transitional measure while structural changes are implemented.

## 5.4 Theoretical Contributions

1. Faculty Load Score (FLS) as a new composite metric
2. Application of service operations theory to Indian coaching institutes
3. Empirical confirmation of supply-demand interaction effects
4. Batch Pressure as a normalized enrollment management measure

## 5.5 Summary

Faculty Load Score dominates delay prediction. Batch Pressure shows threshold effects at BP = 1.05. Their interaction explains 12% additional variance (R2 = 0.9094). Six recommendations provide an evidence-based operational framework.
